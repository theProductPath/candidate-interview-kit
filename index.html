The problem

Interviewers walk in unprepared.

The resume gets forwarded the night before. The interviewer skims it in the elevator. Questions are improvised. Notes are scattered across notebooks and Slack threads. When the hiring committee meets, everyone remembers different things.

The decision is only as good as the preparation. And preparation, for most teams, is an afterthought.

The kit is bespoke for one role, one hiring cycle. It's not a platform. It has no login, no database, no ongoing subscription. Just a folder, an AI skill, and the outputs it generates.
How it works

Three steps. One folder.

1
Prep
Point the skill at a candidate folder. It reads the JD, the interviewer notes, and the resume. It produces a prep brief: competency scores, areas to probe, suggested questions, red flags.
2
Assess
After the interview, share your raw notes with the skill. It structures them into a clean assessment: qualitative scores, evidence, strengths, concerns.
3
Compare
Once you have assessments for all candidates, the skill generates a self-contained comparison tool — all candidates side-by-side. Make your decision. Delete the folder.
What's in the kit

A skill, a folder structure, and a working example.

The kit is an AI skill — a set of instructions any LLM can follow. Claude, ChatGPT, Codex, local models. Point it at your folder and it knows what to do.

The example walks through a complete hiring loop for a fictional Product Analyst role at AirCorp: three candidates, two fully assessed, one still in prep. Everything you need to see how it works before you run it yourself.
