## Your Focus Areas

Based on the hiring manager's direction for your interview, prioritize:
- **Product thinking** — Does James think about user outcomes or just system functionality?
- **Cross-industry adaptability** — Can he transfer Salesforce consolidation patterns to broader platform modernization?
- **Quantitative analysis depth** — Salesforce reporting is there. Can he go deeper?
- **Requirements gathering maturity** — Is his process adaptable beyond Salesforce contexts?

## Recommended Probe Areas

1. **Product vs. platform thinking** — Every example is about making Salesforce work better. Does he think about the user problem behind the platform decision?
2. **Cross-platform curiosity** — Entire career is one vendor. Is there curiosity about other approaches, or is Salesforce the answer to every question?
3. **Requirements beyond known systems** — His gathering process works for Salesforce. What happens when the platform isn't decided yet?

## Suggested Questions

1. "When you consolidated those six Sales Cloud instances, what was the user problem you were solving? Not the technical problem — the user problem."
2. "If a stakeholder came to you with a workflow need and Salesforce wasn't an option, how would you approach it?"
3. "Describe a time your requirements gathering revealed that the original assumption about what to build was wrong."
4. "What's the difference between a good Salesforce implementation and a good product decision?"
5. "How do you decide when a process should be automated versus redesigned?"

## Watch For

- Whether he frames work in terms of user outcomes or platform features
- Curiosity about non-Salesforce approaches — or lack thereof
- How he handles open-ended questions where Salesforce isn't the obvious answer
