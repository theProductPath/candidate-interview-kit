## Your Focus Areas

Based on the hiring manager's direction for your interview, prioritize:
- **Product thinking** — Does Maya think about user outcomes or just process execution? Her EHR work touched clinical workflows — probe whether she was optimizing for users or for documentation completeness.
- **Cross-industry adaptability** — All experience is healthcare. Can she transfer EHR consolidation patterns to SaaS modernization?
- **Quantitative analysis depth** — Resume is qualitative-heavy. Push on data comfort.
- **Requirements gathering maturity** — Process sounds structured. Is it repeatable across different domains?

## Recommended Probe Areas

1. **Quantitative gap** — Resume examples are process maps and stakeholder sessions. Ask for a specific example where data analysis drove a decision.
2. **Cross-industry transfer** — Healthcare-only background. How does she think about applying her consolidation experience to a different technology stack?
3. **Product vs. process orientation** — Was she focused on making the EHR work better for clinicians, or just getting the documentation done?

## Suggested Questions

1. "When you were consolidating those four hospital EHR systems, how did you prioritize which workflows to migrate first? Was that a data-driven decision or stakeholder-driven?"
2. "Give me an example where you used quantitative data — not stakeholder interviews — to make a recommendation."
3. "If you were starting at AirCorp next week, how would you approach understanding a platform you've never worked with before?"
4. "Tell me about a time your requirements gathering process didn't work — what did you change?"
5. "How do you decide when a current-state process should be preserved versus redesigned during migration?"
6. "What's the difference between documenting a workflow and actually improving it?"

## Watch For

- Whether she can articulate product outcomes (user impact) versus process outputs (documentation produced)
- Comfort level discussing data and quantitative methods — does she lean in or deflect?
- How she frames her healthcare experience as transferable — is she aware of the gap or does she assume it translates automatically?
